# Part II Writing Principles

Established during chapter 9 review, April 2026. These five principles apply to all remaining Part II chapters.

## 1. Setting Description
One characterizing image per new location. The image should do narrative work — tell something about the place's character or the POV character's relationship to it. No architectural inventories or catalogues of physical detail.

## 2. Reader Interpretation
Give the image, let it sit. Don't have Elara analyze everything for the reader. Trust the reader to do interpretive work.

## 3. Elara's Part II Arc
She consistently misreads situations through her diplomat's lens. When she interprets, she should be slightly wrong. The reader picks up on the pattern before she does. Dramatic irony through her misreadings.

## 4. Senna's Communication
Always layered. Every letter, dialogue line, or indirect message has two layers minimum. Surface reading is innocent or slightly eccentric. Hidden layer carries operational meaning connected to the Reformist network. Elara doesn't see the hidden layer until the reveal.

## 5. Chapter Endings
Irony over tension. The reader's knowledge creates the tension, not external events or interpersonal standoffs. Elara's wrong certainties are the strongest closing beats.
