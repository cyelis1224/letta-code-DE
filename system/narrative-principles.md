# Narrative Principles — Part II

## Elara's Misreading Pattern (Part II)
Elara consistently interprets border situations through her Thornfield diplomat's lens. When she offers an interpretation, it's slightly wrong. The reader should sense the misread before Elara does. This creates dramatic irony throughout Part II.

## Senna's Communication Rule
Every letter, dialogue line, or indirect message from Senna has two layers:
- **Surface layer:** Innocent, nostalgic, or slightly eccentric
- **Hidden layer:** Operationally coded (Reformist network communication)
Elara doesn't see the hidden layer until the reveal. This applies to ALL Senna communications, always.

## Chapter Ending Preference
End chapters with irony, not external tension. The reader's superior knowledge creates the forward pull. Elara's wrong certainties are stronger closing beats than cliffhangers or thriller beats.

## Setting Description Philosophy
One characterizing image per new location. The image should do narrative work (characterize the place, show POV character's relationship to it). No architectural inventories, no catalogues of physical details.

## Reader Interpretation Principle
Present images without explanatory commentary. Let the reader do the interpretive work. If Elara interprets, keep it brief and filtered through her worldview. Don't narrate meaning — show it.
