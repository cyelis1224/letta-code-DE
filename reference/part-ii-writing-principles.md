# Part II Writing Principles

Established during Chapter 9 review (April 10, 2026). These principles apply to all remaining chapters in Part II (chapters 10-14).

## 1. Setting Description
- **One characterizing image per new location** — not a catalogue of architectural details
- The image should do narrative work: tell something about the place's character or the POV character's relationship to it
- Cut physical inventories (wall colors, flagstone textures, roof tiles, well ropes, etc.)
- Keep only details that serve meaning or character

## 2. Reader Interpretation
- **Present images, don't explain them** — let the reader do the interpretive work
- This is what makes literary fiction literary: the reader is an active participant, not a passenger
- Don't have Elara analyze everything for the reader
- If Elara does interpret, make it brief and slightly wrong

## 3. Elara's Part II Arc
- She consistently misreads situations through her diplomat's lens
- When she interprets, she should be slightly wrong — that's her arc
- She applies Thornfield court logic to border reality and gets it wrong
- The reader picks up the pattern before she does, creating dramatic irony
- She starts recalibrating later in Part II — look for the right moment for the turn

## 4. Senna's Communication
- **Always layered** — every letter, dialogue line, or indirect message has two layers minimum
- Surface reading is always innocent or slightly eccentric
- Hidden layer carries operational meaning connected to the Reformist network
- Elara doesn't see the hidden layer until the reveal
- Senna has been living a double life so long that she doesn't know how to communicate straightforwardly anymore

## 5. Chapter Endings
- **Irony over tension** — the reader's knowledge creates the forward pull
- Elara's wrong certainties are the strongest closing beats
- Contemplation is a rest state that leaves the reader comfortable — avoid this
- Don't use external cliffhangers (mysterious sounds, threatening arrivals) — let the irony do the work
