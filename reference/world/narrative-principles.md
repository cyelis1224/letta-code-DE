# Narrative Principles — Part II

Established during Chapter 9 review (April 2026). These principles guide all Part II drafting.

## Setting Description
- One characterizing image per new location, not a catalogue
- The image must do narrative work (reveal character, relationship to place, or thematic subtext)
- No architectural inventories: wall color, flagstone texture, roof tiles, well ropes — all unless they serve a specific purpose
- Example: Fort Ashmark's half-open gate (institutional decay, border's relationship to authority)

## Reader Interpretation
- Present images without analysis; let the reader make meaning
- If Elara interprets, make it brief and filtered through her diplomat's worldview
- The gap between Elara's reading and reality is where dramatic irony lives
- Don't tell the reader the "correct" interpretation — let them discover it

## Elara's Part II Arc
- Consistently misreads situations through her Thornfield court lens
- She categorizes, she's certain, she's off
- The reader picks up the misreading pattern before she does
- Later in Part II: she begins recalibrating to border reality (exact moment TBD)

## Senna's Communication
- Every letter, dialogue line, or indirect message has two layers minimum
- Surface reading: innocent, slightly eccentric, nostalgic
- Hidden layer: operational meaning connected to Reformist network
- Elara doesn't see the hidden layer until the reveal
- This makes Senna's communication rewarding on reread

## Chapter Endings
- Irony over external tension or interpersonal thriller beats
- Elara's wrong certainties are the strongest closing beats
- The reader's superior knowledge creates forward pull without artificial cliffhangers
- Example: "Fort Ashmark was exactly what she'd expected: a quiet, forgettable posting where nothing of consequence would happen."

## Dialogue Tags
- "Said" and "asked" only
- No creative tags: murmured, whispered, exclaimed, muttered, etc.
